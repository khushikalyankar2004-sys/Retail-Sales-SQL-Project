# Retail Profitability Analysis using SQL

## Project Overview

Retail businesses generate thousands of transactions every day, but raw sales data alone does not provide clear business direction. In this project, SQL is used to analyze a retail sales dataset and convert transactional data into actionable business insights.

The analysis focuses on customer value, product profitability, regional performance, monthly sales trends, discount impact, and customer segmentation. The goal is to demonstrate how SQL can be used to answer real business questions and support data-driven decision making.

---

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to identify the key drivers of revenue and profit by analyzing:

* Top customers by sales
* Most profitable products
* Loss-making products
* Regional and state-wise profitability
* Monthly sales trends
* Discount impact on profit
* Best performing sales representatives
* Customer segmentation based on total sales

---

## Dataset Information

* **Records:** 9,994
* **Columns:** 23
* **Data Includes:** orders, customers, products, sales, profit, quantity, discounts, regions, states, cities, and sales representatives

The dataset represents retail sales transactions and provides a realistic business scenario for SQL-based analysis.

---

## SQL Skills Demonstrated

* Database and table creation
* Data exploration and validation
* Aggregate functions: `SUM()`, `AVG()`, `COUNT()`, `MIN()`, `MAX()`
* `GROUP BY` and `HAVING`
* `ORDER BY` and `LIMIT`
* Window functions: `RANK()`
* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* Views
* Conditional logic using `CASE WHEN`
* Date formatting and monthly trend analysis

---

## Key Analyses Performed

* Calculated total sales and total profit
* Identified top customers by revenue
* Ranked products based on profitability
* Detected duplicate transaction records
* Analyzed monthly sales trends
* Compared profit across regions and states
* Evaluated the relationship between discount and profit
* Identified loss-generating products
* Ranked top-performing sales representatives
* Segmented customers into High, Medium, and Low value groups
* Identified the top customer in each region using CTEs and window functions
* Created a reusable summary view for category performance

---

## Key Business Insights

* High-value customers contribute a significant share of total revenue.
* Some products generate strong sales but low or negative profit.
* Higher discounts can reduce average profitability.
* Profitability varies across regions, states, and cities.
* Monthly sales analysis helps identify business trends over time.
* Customer segmentation helps identify customers who may benefit from loyalty or retention strategies.
